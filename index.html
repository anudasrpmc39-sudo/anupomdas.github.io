About Me

Anupom Das is a Physician and Public Health Enthusiast. He graduated in Medicine (MBBS/MD) from Rangpur Medical College under the University of Rajshahi in 2015 and completed his residential rotatory internship from the academic hospital of the institute. Currently he is pursuing a Joint Master's degree in Health Informatics at Karolinska Institutet in Sweden. The program is a joint masters program of Karolinska Institute offering MMedSc in Medical Science and Stockholm University offering MSc in Computer and System Science. He is an awardee of Swedish Institute Scholarship for Global Professionals (2025-2027), popularly known as, SI Scholarship. Anupom started his career journey as a Research Physician in a 'Multiple Micronutrient Supplementation Study' conducted in Northern Bangladesh by Johns Hopkins University, Baltimore, MD, USA from 2017. He served as a Research Physician there and contributed in implementation of clinical trial on pregnant mother testing the efficacy of multiple micronutrient for improved pregnancy outcome. Then he worked in a 'Randomized Case Control trial for testing the efficacy of Iron fortified Lentil consumption as a medium of Anemia prevention among adolescents'. That project was led by the University of Saskatchewan, Canada. Soon after completion of the project, Anupom joined BRAC James P Grant School of Public Health and led a surveillance team for nationwide NCD surveillance program. He has been enrolled to the MPhil program under the Bangladesh University of Professionals (BUP). In 2022, Anupom started his journey with the national health services being selected by the Civil Service Commission of the country and deployed in the isolation, testing and treatment of the emerging COVID-19 cases then. Then he was deployed to the Management Information System of the Directorate General of Health Services of Bangladesh where his role was implementation, evaluation and analysis of Rapid Detection Test Program of COVID-19. From 2022, he is working as a Medical Officer in the office of the Civil Surgeon, Jashore. Besides, Anupom is currently working as a Fellow of the US-CDC Field Epidemiology Training Program (FETP'B).
