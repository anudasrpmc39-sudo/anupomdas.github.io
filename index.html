Professional Profile

Anupom Das is a Physician and Public Health Enthusiast. He graduated in Medicine (MBBS/MD) from Rangpur Medical College under the University of Rajshahi in 2015 and completed his residential rotatory internship from the academic hospital of the institute.

Currently he is pursuing a Joint Master's degree in Health Informatics at Karolinska Institutet in Sweden. The program is a joint master's program of Karolinska Institute offering MMedSc in Medical Science and Stockholm University offering MSc in Computer and System Science. He is an awardee of Swedish Institute Scholarship for Global Professionals (2025â€“2027), popularly known as the SI Scholarship.

Anupom started his career journey as a Research Physician in a Multiple Micronutrient Supplementation Study conducted in Northern Bangladesh by Johns Hopkins University, Baltimore, USA in 2017. He contributed to the implementation of clinical trials on pregnant mothers testing the efficacy of multiple micronutrients for improved pregnancy outcomes.

He later worked in a Randomized Controlled Trial testing the efficacy of iron-fortified lentil consumption as a medium of anemia prevention among adolescents, led by the University of Saskatchewan, Canada. After completion of the project, he joined BRAC James P Grant School of Public Health and led a surveillance team for nationwide NCD surveillance programs.

In 2020, Anupom joined the national health services after being selected by the Civil Service Commission and was deployed in COVID-19 isolation, testing, and treatment services. He later worked in the Management Information System of the Directorate General of Health Services of Bangladesh, contributing to implementation and evaluation of the Rapid Detection Test Program for COVID-19.

Since 2022, he has been working as a Medical Officer in the office of the Civil Surgeon, Jashore. He is also a Fellow of the US-CDC Field Epidemiology Training Program (FETP-B).
